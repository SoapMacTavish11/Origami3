Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among the Chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Pigeons

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Pandas

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  3. Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

Teddy Bears

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • Washable teddy bears didn't appear until 1954 when the stuffing was switched to an easier to clean nylon fill. Before that, they were stuffed with mohair, wool, and other hard to clean fills.
  • Jackie Miley has the world’s biggest teddy bear collection, totaling 8,026 bear friends in 2012.

Camels

  1. When a camel finds water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  2. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  3. Some calves are born completely white and turn brown as their adult coat comes in.

Cicadas

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
